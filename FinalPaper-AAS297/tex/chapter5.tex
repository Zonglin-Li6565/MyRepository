So, the self-employment has some influence on the children caring, as stated inoverview. However, the determination of children's later success could mostlybe their level of self-control. Further more, self-employed parents canleave more free spaces for children to develop in their own way, and theycan also provide some opportunities for children to interact with thesociety when they are young. Look at the Korean American and self-employmentfrom the view of the whole American society, and their assimilation process, self-employment is much like an interstate other than a final destination.  Forfirst generation Korean immigrants, self-employment is a base stone for their settlement in American, for the success of their later generations.
